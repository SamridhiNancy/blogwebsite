Is mineral oil "SAFE"to be in your cosmetics?

The safety of mineral oil in cosmetics is a topic of debate. Some people believe that it is safe to use, while others believe that it can be harmful.

Mineral oil is a by-product of petroleum and is often used in cosmetics and personal care products as a moisturizer. It forms a barrier on the skin that helps to lock in moisture, which can help to keep skin hydrated.

However, some people believe that mineral oil can clog pores and lead to skin irritation, breakouts, and other skin problems. There is also some concern that mineral oil may be contaminated with harmful impurities, such as polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), which can be carcinogenic.

It's important to note that mineral oil is considered safe by many regulatory agencies, including the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and the European Commission's Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety (SCCS). These agencies have conducted studies on the safety of mineral oil and have concluded that it is safe to use in cosmetics and personal care products.

Ultimately, the safety of mineral oil in cosmetics is a personal decision, and each individual must weigh the benefits and potential risks when making a decision about using mineral oil in their personal care products. It's also important to read product labels and check the ingredients list, as well as research the specific mineral oil used in the product.
